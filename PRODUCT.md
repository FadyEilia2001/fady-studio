# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Small and medium business owners — mostly **non-technical**. They run a real business (a shop, a clinic, a service, a growing brand) and feel friction: manual busywork, a website that doesn't convert, no app, a reputation that lags behind how good they actually are. They arrive skeptical (they've been burned by cheap freelancers and template agencies) and time-poor. The job to be done: *figure out, fast, whether this person can build the thing that makes my business work better — and whether I'd trust them to do it.*

The site is bilingual (English + Arabic, RTL-aware), so a meaningful share of visitors read right-to-left. Both languages are first-class, not a translation afterthought.

## Product Purpose

A personal studio/portfolio for **Fady Eilia**, who builds custom apps & websites, automation tooling, and brand/reputation work for businesses. The site exists to convert a skeptical SMB owner into a lead by making one impression land hard: *this person is clearly excellent, and clearly not a commodity freelancer.* Success = the visitor remembers the site, trusts the craft on sight, and reaches out. Being memorable is the primary job; clarity of offer serves it, not the other way around.

## Brand Personality

**Playful. Inventive. Human.** — executed **boldly and expressively**, never quietly. The voice is direct and plain-spoken (no agency jargon, no enterprise stiffness): it talks to a busy business owner like a sharp, creative person who happens to be technical. Energy and wit are welcome; the work should feel made by a human with taste, not generated by a template. Emotional goal: *delight + confidence* — the visitor enjoys the page and comes away certain this person ships dependable, high-quality work.

The tension to hold on purpose: maximal visual boldness (big type, decisive motion, committed color) carrying a warm, human, slightly playful voice. Bold is the volume; playful-human is the tone. If a choice reads as "impressive but cold," it's wrong; if it reads as "fun but flimsy," it's also wrong.

## Anti-references

Explicitly avoid all of these — several were named directly:

- **Generic SaaS template**: gradient-blob hero, three identical feature cards, Inter/Roboto, purple-to-blue gradient, hero-metric row. The default AI/template look.
- **Cheap freelancer site**: stock-photo handshakes, clip-art service icons, "I do everything, cheap" clutter.
- **Corporate / sterile**: stiff, gray, soulless enterprise layouts with no personality.
- **Overdesigned / gimmicky**: effects piled on for their own sake, hard to read, slow, style over substance.
- **The standard studio / graphic-design website**: the saturated "creative studio" template — oversized centered serif, marquee of client logos, identical project grid, "we are a multidisciplinary studio" energy. This is the second-order trap; avoid the whole lane, not just the first reflex.

## Design Principles

1. **Memorable beats tidy.** The win condition is "I remember that site," not "that was clean." When a safe choice and a distinctive choice are both defensible, take the distinctive one — then make it work flawlessly.
2. **Bold execution, human voice.** Push scale, motion, and color hard; keep the words warm, plain, and a little playful. The contrast between loud visuals and a human tone is the brand.
3. **Show the work works.** This visitor is non-technical and skeptical. Prove competence through the craft of the site itself and through concrete outcomes — not adjectives, not jargon, not buzzword stacks.
4. **Not-a-studio-template.** Actively reject the saturated "creative studio" and SaaS grammar (centered serif hero, eyebrow on every section, identical card grids, logo marquees as proof). If a section looks like it could be lifted onto any other portfolio, redesign it.
5. **Two languages, one quality bar.** Arabic/RTL is a first-class layout, not a mirror afterthought — typography, rhythm, and motion must feel native in both directions.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **WCAG 2.2 AA** as the floor: body text ≥4.5:1, large/UI text ≥3:1, including text over the hero imagery and over the dark navy surface.
- **Reduced motion is non-negotiable.** Every Framer Motion entrance, scroll reveal, and hover has a `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` alternative (crossfade or instant). Reveals must enhance already-visible content, never gate it.
- **Keyboard + focus**: visible focus states on all interactive elements; logical tab order.
- **RTL parity**: Arabic layout, focus order, and motion direction are correct and tested, not assumed.
